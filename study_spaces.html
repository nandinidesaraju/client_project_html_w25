Study Spaces on Campus

Overview

The University of Michigan offers a variety of study spaces across campus to cater to different learning styles and preferences. Whether you prefer quiet libraries, collaborative workspaces, or cozy corners with natural light, thereâ€™s a spot for everyone.

Popular Study Locations

  • Hatcher Graduate Library: A historic library with quiet reading rooms and access to extensive resources.
  • Duderstadt Center: Ideal for group projects and multimedia work, equipped with state-of-the-art technology.
  • Shapiro Undergraduate Library (UgLi): A mix of quiet and collaborative spaces with extended hours.
  • Trotter Multicultural Center: Offers a welcoming environment for studying and community events.
  • Ross School of Business: Features modern, open spaces for both solo and group work.

Tips for Effective Study

To make the most of your study time, consider the following tips:

  • Find a space that matches your study needs (quiet vs. collaborative).
  • Plan your visit during less crowded hours if you prefer peace and quiet.
  • Utilize available resources like Wi-Fi, charging stations, and printers.
  • Stay organized by bringing all necessary materials, and take regular breaks.
