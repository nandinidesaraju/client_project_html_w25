Study Spaces on Campus

Overview

Finding the right study space can make a huge difference in productivity and focus. The University of Michigan offers a variety of study environments, from silent libraries to lively collaboration hubs. Whether you need a distraction-free zone or a space to brainstorm with classmates, there’s a perfect spot for you on campus.

Below, you'll find recommendations for some of the best study spots, along with useful tips to maximize your efficiency while studying.

Popular Study Locations

  • Hatcher Graduate Library: A historic library with quiet reading rooms, group study areas, and extensive academic resources.
  • Duderstadt Center ("The Dude"): Perfect for engineering and art students, with collaborative workspaces, advanced technology, and creative studios.
  • Shapiro Undergraduate Library (UgLi): A go-to space for undergraduates, offering flexible study areas, reservable rooms, and late-night hours.
  • Trotter Multicultural Center: A welcoming and inclusive space for studying, relaxing, and engaging with the community.
  • Ross School of Business: Features sleek, modern study spaces, many with great views and comfortable seating.
  • Law Library: One of the most aesthetically pleasing and silent study spaces, ideal for deep concentration.
  • Taubman Health Sciences Library: A great option for students in health-related fields, offering study rooms and medical research resources.

Finding the Right Study Space

Choosing the best study environment depends on your personal preferences and academic needs. Here are some things to consider when selecting a study space:

  • Noise Level: Need absolute silence? Try the Law Library or Hatcher’s designated quiet zones. Prefer background noise? The UgLi or Ross Atrium might be better.
  • Seating Comfort: Ross and Duderstadt provide ergonomic seating, while Trotter Multicultural Center offers cozy lounge areas.
  • Access to Outlets: If you’ll be studying for long hours, check for locations with ample charging stations, such as the Duderstadt Center.
  • Study Hours: Need a late-night study session? The UgLi and Duderstadt Center have extended hours.
  • Food and Coffee: If you like snacking while studying, consider spaces near cafes like Shapiro, Ross, or Pierpont Commons.

Tips for Effective Studying

Maximize your productivity with these study strategies:

  • Choose a Study Method: Try the Pomodoro technique (25-minute study sessions with 5-minute breaks) or active recall for better retention.
  • Set Specific Goals: Outline what you want to accomplish during each study session to stay on track.
  • Limit Distractions: Use noise-canceling headphones or apps like Forest to stay focused.
  • Stay Hydrated & Take Breaks: Short breaks and proper hydration can boost concentration.
  • Utilize Campus Resources: Book study rooms in advance, use whiteboards for brainstorming, and check out the library’s research databases.

Study Space Reservation & Additional Resources

Many study spaces allow for room reservations to ensure you have a dedicated workspace. To book a study room, visit:

  • University Library Study Spaces
  • Ross Business School Room Reservations
  • College of Engineering Study Rooms

For additional resources, including tutoring and academic coaching, check out the Sweetland Center for Writing or the Academic Success Program.
