Physical Health

Keeping your body healthy is essential for success. A healthy lifestyle improves focus, reduces stress, and enhances overall academic performance. The University of Michigan offers numerous resources to help you stay in top physical condition:

University Health Service (UHS)

University Health Service (UHS) provides comprehensive medical care, including routine check-ups, vaccinations, specialist referrals, and urgent care services. Students also have access to nutrition counseling, travel health consultations, and confidential health advising.

Recreational Sports

Participate in various fitness programs, intramural sports, and recreational activities. UM provides state-of-the-art gym facilities, yoga and meditation sessions, swimming pools, and outdoor adventure programs to keep you active and engaged.

Mental Health

Maintaining your mental well-being is crucial for thriving in graduate school. Managing coursework, research, and personal life can be challenging, but there are resources to support you:

Counseling and Psychological Services (CAPS)

Access individual counseling, group therapy, workshops, and crisis intervention services. CAPS also provides outreach programs and online self-help tools to support students dealing with anxiety, depression, and academic stress.

Wellness Coaching

Work one-on-one with a wellness coach to develop personalized strategies for managing stress, improving sleep habits, enhancing time management skills, and creating a balanced lifestyle.

Mindfulness Resources

Engage in guided mindfulness and meditation sessions designed to help students cultivate relaxation and focus. UM provides in-person and virtual mindfulness workshops for stress relief.

Support Groups

Join peer-led support groups for shared experiences and collective coping strategies. Topics range from academic pressure to adjusting to life in a new city, helping students navigate the challenges of graduate school.

Life Outside the Classroom

Graduate school is more than just academics. It's important to cultivate a balanced life and find joy outside of studying. Here are some resources to help you make the most of your time at UM:

  • Student Organizations: Join one of the 1,600+ student organizations that focus on academic, professional, cultural, and social interests. These groups provide an excellent way to make friends, network, and develop leadership skills.
  • Arts and Culture: Attend concerts, theatre performances, art exhibitions, and film screenings. UM hosts numerous cultural events that celebrate diversity and creativity.
  • Career Services: Access career counseling, resume workshops, job fairs, and internship placements. The UM Career Center connects students with employers and professional development opportunities.
  • Community Engagement: Get involved with local volunteering opportunities and service projects. Make a difference by participating in initiatives focused on education, sustainability, and social justice.
  • Housing and Dining: Find comfortable and convenient living arrangements, including university housing and off-campus rentals. Explore diverse dining options that cater to different dietary needs.
